Bank Churn Analysis Dashboard
1. Business Problem

Banks often lose customers due to poor service experience, better competitor offers, lack of engagement, or product dissatisfaction.
Customer churn directly affects profitability because acquiring a new customer costs more than retaining an existing one.

The challenge for the bank is to:

Identify customers who are likely to leave
Understand churn patterns
Find key factors influencing customer attrition
Improve customer retention strategies

2. Goal of the Dashboard

The main goal of this dashboard is to:

Monitor overall customer churn performance
Identify churn rate across different customer segments
Analyze churn by age, gender, country, and account activity
Understand the relationship between products and churn
Help management make data-driven retention decisions

3. Key Visuals Used in Dashboard
KPI Cards

Used for:

Total Customers
Lost Customers
Churn Rate

Why used:
KPI cards quickly show the most important business metrics at a glance.

Donut Charts

Used for:

Customer by Gender
Country-wise Customers
Active vs Non-active Customers
Credit Card Holder
Churned vs Unchurned Customers

Why used:
Donut charts help compare proportions and percentage contribution of customer groups.

Column Chart

Used for:

Total customers by country and churn

Why used:
Column charts compare customer counts between countries and churn categories.

Line + Bar Combination Chart

Used for:

Customer churn rate by age group

Why used:
This visual shows both:

Number of customers
Churn rate trend by age

This helps identify which age group has higher churn.

Stacked Bar Chart

Used for:

Churn rate by gender and country

Why used:
Stacked bars show how churn differs across multiple dimensions in one chart.

4. Business Impact

This dashboard can help the bank to:

Reduce customer attrition
Improve customer satisfaction
Identify high-risk customer groups
Increase customer retention
Improve profitability
Support strategic business decisions

By using this dashboard, decision-makers can focus on customers who are more likely to churn and take preventive actions.

5. Key Insights Derived from Dashboard
Insight 1

The overall churn rate is 20.37%, meaning 1 out of every 5 customers is leaving the bank.

Insight 2

Customers in the 50–60 age group show the highest churn rate compared to other age segments.

Insight 3

Inactive customers have a significantly higher churn tendency than active customers.

Insight 4

Customers without a credit card are more likely to churn.

Insight 5

Among countries:

France has the highest customer base
Churn behavior varies across countries
Female customers show slightly higher churn in some regions
Insight 6

Customer churn differs by:

Gender
Geography
Product usage
Account activity

This indicates churn is influenced by multiple business factors.
